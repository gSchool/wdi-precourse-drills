Bio

I was born and raised in North Carolina. After finishing high school I took off to Southern Illinois to study Digital Media with an emphasis in Audio Recording. In college I found a love for a lot of different things such as art, theater, web developement, computer programming, sound design, and electronics like modular synthesis and so much more.

After graduating, I moved back to North Carolina and found myself practicing graphic design and finally landing a job. I spent almost 5 years working professionally in the Adobe Creative Suite. I grew to want more, so...I moved out west to Santa Fe, New Mexico. Santa Fe is one of the most art-rich cities in the United States. My time there was a struggle with not a whole lot of opportunity. However I was given the opportunity to work with the talented people at Meow Wolf, where they showed me the true power of programming. This is where my love for coding began.

Since my time in Santa Fe was not progressing like I had hoped, I decided to move to a larger city with a bit more growth. About 6 hours north, Denver, Colorado seemed like a good fit. At the moment I am studing to be a Web Developer at Galvanize, a bootcamp for those aspiring to be programmers in the Denver area.

New Mexico

I lived in Santa Fe, New Mexico from July 2015 to March 2017. During that time I learned a lot about its people, culture, and land. It had a huge impact on me; so much so that I began to write a musical album based off of the state. The album intertwines autobiographical events with New Mexico myth and folklore.
