How did I get here...

Growing up I was the quintessential middle child, fiercely independent. I have an older sister (Kera) and a younger brother (Kole).

Friends help shape who you are, how you think about the world, and truly become like family. I have made some great friends since moving to Denver – friends who are always up for an adventure and a nice, chilled glass of Rosé.

There is no shortage of activities to enjoy in Denver, and personally I try to fill my free time with as many as I can. Whether I am climbing my first Fourteener, shredding down a mountain on my snowboard, or rafting with friends; I am happiest when outside.

Growing up in a deeply religious Southern Baptist household, with parents who had been together since middle school, made for quite the interesting childhood. When I was 9 years old I found out that my mother had breast cancer, something she has been battling ever since. To protect my sister, brother and I, our family simply did not discuss this. Because of this avoidance, I did not truly come to terms with the severity of my mother’s illness until around fourth grade.

Years later, I finally came out to my parents when they found a book in my bedroom by Richard Dawkins, a prominent atheist, called ‘The Selfish Gene’. Their first response was anger and disappointment in me for going against their religion. They called me stupid because I didn't believe in the same things that they did. Fortunately, they eventually came around to accept me the best that they could, and now support me in all that I do.

During my undergraduate at the University of Louisville, I had the privilege of playing catcher for four years for the Louisville Cardinals, a top 10 nationally ranked baseball team. Most recently, I founded a new venture called Tails, a pet sitting business here in the Mile High City. Tails is made successful primarily because of great friends, and my client referral program.
